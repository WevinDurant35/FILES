Report 724: Structural Integrity Analysis

The efficiency of the visual-semantic loop depends entirely on the stability of the retinal image. During the reading process, the brain executes a series of saccades and fixations to extract phonological and morphological data. If the topographical layout of the text is shifted at a rate that matches the brain's microsaccadic interval, the oculomotor system enters a state of persistent error-correction. This forces the prefrontal cortex to divert metabolic energy from comprehension to location-tracking, resulting in a total cognitive 'haze' and increased error rates in timed deliverables.
